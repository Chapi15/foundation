Travel and Hotels

A quick primer for those traveling to Mumbai.

Mumbai

Mumbai has a fascinating history with plenty of interesting stories, and people of undaunting spirits

Things to Do

  • There are lots of places to visit from ancient Buddhist caves to British monuments across the city.
  • You can enjoy the view of the sea at Bandstand at Bandra and Marine Drive at Churchgate.
  • A culinary experience like no other, Mumbai hosts many amazing Cafes and Bars.

Hotels to Stay

  • Hotels in Mumbai are generally expensive, so avoid staying very close to the venue. There are a lot of hotels around the airport and that might be the best option for getting a deal.
  • The venue would be around 30-45 mins for the airport based on where you are.
  • Cleartrip might have the best deals. Airbnb might also have good options.

Getting Around

  • Uber and Ola Cabs are app based taxi services that will work very well. We 100% recommend these over local taxis and auto-rickshaws (tuk-tuks) which are likely to over-charge out station passengers.
  • If you do travel by local taxis or auto-rickshaws, they are not allowed to charge a fare greater than the one calculated by the meter after the trip.
  • If you are up for some adventure, you can try the Mumbai local trains. Here's a guide for that.

Venue

  • The venue is at the Mysore Association Auditorium and has all modern facilities you would expect from a venue. It is centrally located and easily accessible from all corners.
